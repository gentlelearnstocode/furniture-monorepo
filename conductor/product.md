# Product Definition: ThienAn Furniture Monorepo

## Initial Concept
A modern, full-stack furniture e-commerce platform built with Next.js 16, Turborepo, and PostgreSQL.

## Target Users
- **End Customers:** Individual shoppers looking for furniture and home decor. The platform is designed to provide them with a seamless browsing and purchasing experience.

## Primary Goals
- **Drive Sales:** Provide a seamless e-commerce experience to convert visitors into customers through an intuitive storefront.
- **Streamline Operations:** Enable efficient management of complex product hierarchies and site content via a powerful admin portal, reducing the overhead for staff.

## Key Features
- **Advanced Product Discovery:** Implementation of hierarchical catalogs, robust search functionality, and curated collections to help users find exactly what they need.
- **Integrated Admin Dashboard:** A comprehensive suite of tools for managing products, blogs, services, and global site settings from a single interface.
- **Dynamic Storefront:** A responsive, SEO-optimized web interface featuring dynamic content such as hero sections and featured products to engage visitors.

## Success Metrics
- **Conversion Rate:** Measuring the percentage of visitors who transition to making a purchase or a direct inquiry.
- **Management Efficiency:** Tracking the reduction in time and effort required for administrative staff to maintain and update the platform's content.
- **Site Performance:** Monitoring load times and SEO rankings to ensure a high-quality user experience and strong organic reach.
- **User Engagement:** Evaluating user interaction with secondary content like blog posts, service showcases, and project portfolios.

## Design Principles
- **Modern & Minimalist:** A clean and uncluttered aesthetic that prioritizes the visual appeal of the furniture products.
- **User-Centric:** Focus on intuitive navigation and frictionless workflows for both the end customers and the administrative users.
- **Scalable & Maintainable:** Leveraging robust architectural patterns (monorepo, shared packages) to support future growth and simplify long-term maintenance.
